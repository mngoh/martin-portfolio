How I Completed My Undergraduate Degree in 2.5 Years

Coming into college in 2018, I already had a good idea that I wanted to graduate early. Fortunately, I already had 30 credits coming from high school through AP and Dual Enrollment courses.

Graduation Timeline

Freshman Year - 2018

I started at Virginia Commonwealth University (VCU) in August of 2018. I started out with the goal of pursuing an undergraduate degree in Finance but then changed to Supply Chain management and Analytics. I was also on the men's soccer team while at VCU.

In the winter of 2018-2019 I completed a course that gave me 3 credits.

Sophomore Year - 2019

As a sophomore, I had gotten into the routine of both school and soccer. I started off strong in the fall achieving a 4.0 GPA.

It was going very well until Spring of 2020. That changed a lot of things... During the Spring semester I took 18 credits and in the summer I took 9 credits including an internship.

Junior/Senior Year - 2020

My final fall at VCU consisted of practicing with the men's soccer team and taking my final courses in Supply Chain Management and Analytics.

It was a bitter sweet end to my time at VCU because most things were closed on campus due to COVID.

Conclusion

Overall, graduating with my Bachelor's Degree in Business with a Concentration in Supply Chain Management and Analytics, was a major milestone on in my career and I'm grateful for the opportunity.

Hopefully you can find inspiration in my journey to pursue your goals, no matter what they are. The journey is never easy but the ultimate goal is very fulfilling.
